Software of the Week: EMACS

Emacs is a family of text-editors based off of the GNU project, well known within the enthusiast and business sectors of the computer world. As everyone starts from the ubiquitous NANO and probably even had their start with EDIT.COM (Remember how you could use that within 32-bit windows?), it's important to remember the one text-editor which could hypothetically rule them all: Emacs. Developed by David A Moon and Guy L Steele in the 1970s, it was programmed in C and LISP and has been widely supported across a large variety of Operating Systems. Ultimately, the desktop is your oyster with this versatile and powerful software.
